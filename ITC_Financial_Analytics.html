03
Six Insights — Ruthlessly Data-Backed
INSIGHT 01 / FMCG-OTHERS TRANSFORMATION
FMCG–Others EBIT grew from ₹423.05 Cr (FY2020) → ₹1,778.55 Cr (FY2024) — a 5-year CAGR of +30.1%. Revenue grew from ₹12,813.73 Cr to ₹20,922.47 Cr (+13.3% CAGR). This means profitability is growing more than twice as fast as revenue — operating leverage is kicking in after 15 years of scale investment. FY2025 showed a margin dip to 7.2% (from 8.5%) on ₹21,975 Cr revenue — a temporary setback, not a reversal.
Implication: The thesis that ITC's consumer business was a "money pit" is now definitively broken. At ₹1,778 Cr EBIT on ₹20,922 Cr revenue, this is the fastest-scaling segment in the group. A standalone FMCG company at this scale (₹20K+ Cr revenue, 8%+ EBIT, growing 13%+ CAGR) would command a valuation north of ₹60,000–80,000 Cr. It is materially undervalued inside the ITC conglomerate structure.
INSIGHT 02 / PPP STRUCTURAL COLLAPSE
Paperboards, Paper & Packaging peaked at ₹2,293.99 Cr EBIT (31.4% margin) in FY2023. Two years later in FY2025, EBIT stands at ₹911.49 Cr (13.8% margin) — a collapse of ₹1,382.50 Cr in absolute EBIT (-60.3%). Revenue is essentially flat (₹7,304 Cr → ₹6,626 Cr). Margins are being crushed by cheap Chinese paperboard imports, global paper market oversupply, and softening packaging demand from downstream FMCG clients.
Implication: FY2023 was an anomaly driven by post-COVID packaging demand surge and commodity pricing. The "real" steady-state margin for this segment is likely 15–22%, not 31%. The FY2025 level of 13.8% is now below the 5-year average (24.9%) by 11 percentage points. Without a Chinese dumping duty action or a structural demand recovery, this segment will continue compressing ITC's consolidated EBIT.
INSIGHT 03 / CIGARETTE SEGMENT CONCENTRATION RISK
FMCG–Cigarettes contributed ₹51,885.30 Cr (78.5%) of cumulative group EBIT over FY2022–FY2024. In FY2025 alone, it generated ₹20,024.87 Cr EBIT on ₹32,631.27 Cr revenue. Its EBIT margin has been remarkably stable: 70.1% (FY2020) → 62.6% (FY2021, COVID) → 63.4% → 63.6% → 62.4% → 61.4% (FY2025). This is a slow but real margin decline of ~880 bps over 5 years.
Implication: The cigarette segment is funding every rupee of investment in FMCG-Others, Hotels (now demerged), and Agri. This is a classic "cash cow funding growth" structure — healthy if the growth bets pay off. But there is a structural existential risk: every percentage point of margin decline here wipes out the entire EBIT of Hotels or a year of Agri earnings. ITC has exactly 5–8 years to fully monetize FMCG-Others before tobacco volumes structurally decline.
INSIGHT 04 / HOTELS DEMERGER — TEXTBOOK VALUE UNLOCK
Hotels went from ₹-534.91 Cr EBIT (FY2021, COVID) to ₹753.77 Cr EBIT and 25.3% margin (FY2024) — the best performance in the segment's history. ITC then demerged it as ITC Hotels Ltd, listed separately. Total Hotels revenue grew from ₹623.59 Cr (FY2021) to ₹2,973.74 Cr (FY2024) — a +376% recovery in 3 years.
Implication: This was a perfectly timed demerger — done at peak operational performance, not in distress. ITC extracted maximum value for existing shareholders by listing Hotels when margins were at 25.3% (hospitality sector premium). The demerger removes ₹6.5K Cr of assets and ₹2.2K Cr of liabilities from ITC's books, cleaning up the capital structure for focused FMCG+Agri+PPP operations.
INSIGHT 05 / AGRI BUSINESS — HIGH REVENUE, THIN MARGIN, EXTREME VOLATILITY
Agri revenue swung: ₹5,904 Cr (FY2020) → ₹12,126 Cr (FY2022, +105% in 2 years) → ₹8,417 Cr (FY2024, -31%) → ₹12,065 Cr (FY2025, +43%). These are not operational changes — they are commodity price cycles. Yet EBIT has been remarkably stable: range of ₹788–1,478 Cr with margins between 8.5–14.9%. Agri is a volume-volatile, margin-stable business.
Implication: Agri Business should not be evaluated on revenue CAGR — it is meaningless for a commodities trading + processing operation. The correct lens is EBIT per unit capital deployed and EBIT margin stability. On those metrics, Agri is actually a decent business — delivering ₹1,000–1,500 Cr EBIT on ₹4,000–7,000 Cr segment assets. The risk is tail-risk commodity price shocks, not structural decline.
INSIGHT 06 / RAW MATERIAL — THE REAL MARGIN DESTROYER
Raw material costs grew from ₹13,121.76 Cr (FY2020) → ₹23,440.12 Cr (FY2025) — a 5-year CAGR of 12.3%, faster than group revenue CAGR of 9.7%. Total raw material increase over 5 years: +₹10,318.36 Cr. This is the single largest driver of group-level margin pressure. ITC's biggest raw materials include tobacco leaf, wheat (Aashirvaad), palm oil (foods), wood pulp (PPP), and packaging materials — all subject to global commodity cycles.
Implication: ITC cannot control global commodity prices. It can only manage through: (a) long-term procurement contracts and backward integration, (b) premiumisation to pass costs to consumers, (c) portfolio mix shift toward higher-margin segments. The fact that FMCG-Others margins still expanded from 3.3% to 8.5% despite raw material inflation is the clearest evidence that the premiumisation strategy is working.

ACCELERATE INVESTMENT
FMCG – Others
Reallocate 35–40% of cigarette cash flows into FMCG-Others capex. EBIT CAGR of 30%+ and consistent margin expansion from 3.3% → 8.5% are signals of genuine operating leverage. The FY2025 margin dip to 7.2% is input cost related, not structural. Accelerate premiumisation of Aashirvaad, Sunfeast, and Fiama. Target: ₹3,000 Cr EBIT by FY2028 (from ₹1,579 Cr in FY2025).

URGENT RESTRUCTURE
Paperboards, P&P
EBIT collapsed from ₹2,293.99 Cr (FY2023) to ₹911.49 Cr (FY2025) — ₹1,382 Cr destroyed in 2 years. Three actions required: (1) File anti-dumping petition on Chinese paperboard imports with DGTR. (2) Shift capacity mix toward specialty/sustainable packaging where Chinese competition is weaker. (3) Renegotiate long-term supply contracts with FMCG-Others to lock in captive offtake at fair margins. No new greenfield capex until margin recovers to 20%+.

Agri Business
Agri is a commodity business that will always show revenue volatility (-31% to +43% in consecutive years). Management decision should be evaluated on EBIT per rupee of assets deployed, not revenue trend. At ₹1,478 Cr EBIT on ₹6,956 Cr assets, the return is decent. No major capital allocation change warranted. Focus on leaf tobacco backward integration and specialty spices value-add which carry structural margin advantage.

STRATEGIC NOTE — CIGARETTES
Protect the Cash Engine at All Costs — But Acknowledge the Clock
The cigarette segment generated ₹20,024.87 Cr EBIT in FY2025. Its margin has declined from 70.1% (FY2020) to 61.4% (FY2025) — 870 bps over 5 years. This is slow, but it is directional. ITC has approximately 5–10 years before tobacco-linked regulatory pressure, volume softening, and health-conscious consumer shift materially impairs this segment. Every rupee of FMCG-Others EBIT built in this window is the structural hedge. The board must explicitly acknowledge the tobacco dependency and have a published 10-year transition roadmap. The demerger of Hotels is the first step. Spinning off Agri and PPP as separate listed entities over the next 3–5 years would unlock the full NAV of the conglomerate — estimated at ₹700–900 Cr per share vs. current market value — and allow FMCG-Others to be valued as a standalone consumer company.

05
Conclusion & Key Findings
Overall Conclusion
ITC Limited's FY2020–FY2025 financials tell a story of one engine powering a transformation, one segment collapsing, one quietly becoming profitable, one getting cleaned up, and one riding commodity cycles. The cigarette business (₹20,024 Cr EBIT, 61.4% margin in FY2025) is funding one of the most ambitious brand portfolios ever built by an Indian FMCG company — but it is doing so against a structural headwind that every ITC analyst and board member must acknowledge openly.

The FMCG-Others transformation from ₹423 Cr EBIT (FY2020) to ₹1,779 Cr EBIT (FY2024) at a 30.1% CAGR is not talked about enough. It is the most important internal financial development at ITC in the last decade — the actual proof that 15 years of brand investment is converting into real, scalable profitability.

The demerger of Hotels at peak operational performance (25.3% margin, FY2024) was strategically impeccable. The collapse of Paperboards, Paper & Packaging from ₹2,294 Cr to ₹911 Cr EBIT in two years is the most urgent operational problem in the group right now. And Agri Business, while superficially volatile, is actually a disciplined operation delivering consistent ₹800–1,500 Cr EBIT despite extreme commodity swings.

The strategic thesis for ITC over the next 5–8 years is clear: monetise the cigarette cash flows into FMCG-Others at maximum speed, structurally fix PPP, maintain Agri without over-capitalising, and create a path to full FMCG conglomerate demerger that unlocks the ₹700–900+ per share NAV currently obscured by the tobacco discount.
Key Findings — Data Verified from Annual Reports
  • FMCG–Others EBIT CAGR: +30.1% (FY2020→FY2024). From ₹423 Cr to ₹1,779 Cr in 4 years.
  • Cigarettes: 78.5% of cumulative group EBIT (FY2022–FY2024). Margin stable at 61–70% across 6 years.
  • PPP EBIT collapsed -60.3% from FY2023 peak (₹2,294 Cr) to FY2025 (₹911 Cr). Structural issue.
  • Hotels swung from -₹534.91 Cr EBIT (FY2021) to +₹753.77 Cr (FY2024) before demerger.
  • Agri Business revenue volatility range: -31.6% (FY2024) to +54.2% (FY2022). EBIT remarkably stable.
  • Raw material CAGR: 12.3% (FY2020→FY2025) — fastest growing cost, exceeding group revenue CAGR of 9.7%.
  • Group revenue grew from ₹46,323 Cr (FY2020) to ₹73,465 Cr (FY2025) — CAGR of 9.7%.
  • Advertising spend peaked at ₹1,439.45 Cr (FY2024), then cut to ₹1,319.94 Cr (FY2025) — Hotels demerger effect.
  • Employee cost CAGR: only 5.1% — the best-managed cost line in the group across 5 years.
  • Hotels demerger (FY2025) removed ₹6,548.66 Cr assets and ₹1,128.07 Cr liabilities from ITC balance sheet.

Key Assumptions
  • All figures: ₹ Crores (1 Cr = 10 Million INR). Fiscal year: April 1 to March 31.
  • profit = Segment Results (EBIT) as stated in ITC Annual Reports. NOT PAT. Income tax is group-level unallocated.
  • expenses = Segment Revenue (External) − Segment EBIT. ITC does not publish segment-level cost breakdown.
  • group_costs table uses company-level standalone P&L cost data — not allocated to segments.
  • Hotels excluded from FY2025 data: demerged as ITC Hotels Ltd, treated as discontinued operations in FY2025 report.
  • FY2025 Agri figures reflect continuing operations only; some inter-segment numbers restated post-Hotels demerger.
  • Revenue = External segment revenue only (inter-segment eliminated). Total excludes eliminations.
  • CAGR for Hotels computed FY2020–FY2024 only (4-year, 4 observations, last data point before demerger).
